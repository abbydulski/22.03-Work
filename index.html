1. Introduction

  • Tasked with improving the current data logger system used by the Blackfeet Indian Reservation in northern Montana
  • The reservation uses the data loggers for research purposes
  • Data loggers are typically set up facing each other 15 ft apart
  • Components of the current logger are rebar, a camera trap, an audio recorder, and batteries
  • Current system is inefficient and laborous
  • Using solar panels, a voltage booster, and soldering a cutting of the components wires we were able to come up with a new system resolving many of the complications of the current one
  • New system components are rebar, a camera trap, an audio recorder, a solar panel pack, and a portable charger

2. Problem Statement

We aim to address the problems below.

  • The current system is not easy to assemble and carry
  • The current system is not safe from animals and bad weather
  • The current system requires battery maintenance
  • Battery maintenance requires numerous treks to the data logger every recording period
  • The treks are unsafe to the researchers doing them

3. Project Goals

Our goals for the project are below. Completion of our goals will prevent the need for frequent visits to the site to replace batteries and it will make sure the equipment is not damaged by animals or bad weather.

  • Integrate a self-powered mechanism for the data logger
  • Design a more robust carrier of the equipment

4. Project Timeline

A rough timeline of our porject is below:

  • Prioritize issues with the data logger
  • Design the mechanical structure to meet this goal
  • Design the electronic structure to meet this goal
  • Test the designs
  • Commit and build out the best design

Key milestones in the project are below:
  • 3D printing of the camera and audio holder
  • Analysis of the power needs of a circuit connecting the system of components
  • Integration of the battery and the solar panel

5 Process and Methodology. Project Goals

A description of the process followed to build the project is below:

  • Analyzed the circuit of the camera to uncover it needs 12V of power
  • Analyzed the circuit of the audio component to unvocer it needs 5V of power
  • Analyzed the power privided by the solar panel and the portable charger
  • Analyzed the amount of current procuded by the solar panel

Methodologies and tools used are below:
  • Fusion 360 for design of parts
  • Original Prusa i3 3D printer
  • Soldered electronic components
